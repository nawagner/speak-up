## Component Breakdown

### 1. Pronunciation (35 points)

**Excellent (32-35 points)**
- Near-native or native pronunciation
- Correct phonetic sounds including nasal vowels (on, an, in, un)
- Proper liaison and elision
- Accurate intonation patterns
- Clear distinction between similar sounds (u/ou, é/è/ê)

**Good (28-31 points)**
- Generally accurate pronunciation with minor errors
- Most nasal vowels correct
- Liaison attempted and mostly correct
- Intonation generally appropriate
- Occasional confusion between similar sounds

**Satisfactory (24-27 points)**
- Understandable pronunciation with noticeable accent
- Some nasal vowel errors
- Inconsistent liaison
- Flat or exaggerated intonation
- Regular confusion between similar sounds

**Needs Improvement (20-23 points)**
- Heavy accent interfering with comprehension
- Frequent phonetic errors
- Little to no liaison
- Incorrect stress patterns
- Significant difficulty with French-specific sounds

**Unsatisfactory (0-19 points)**
- Pronunciation severely impedes communication
- Consistent inability to produce French phonemes
- No attempt at proper intonation or liaison

---

### 2. Grammar (35 points)

**Excellent (32-35 points)**
- Consistently correct verb conjugations across tenses
- Proper agreement (gender, number, subject-verb)
- Accurate use of pronouns and articles
- Complex sentence structures with subordinate clauses
- Correct use of subjunctive and conditional moods

**Good (28-31 points)**
- Generally accurate verb conjugations with minor errors
- Most agreements correct
- Appropriate pronoun and article usage
- Some complex structures attempted
- Occasional errors in subjunctive/conditional

**Satisfactory (24-27 points)**
- Basic verb conjugations mostly correct
- Frequent agreement errors that don't impede meaning
- Simple sentence structures
- Limited use of complex grammar
- Avoidance of difficult tenses

**Needs Improvement (20-23 points)**
- Frequent verb conjugation errors
- Consistent agreement problems
- Very simple sentence structures only
- Incorrect pronoun usage
- Limited tense variety

**Unsatisfactory (0-19 points)**
- Pervasive grammatical errors
- Inability to conjugate basic verbs
- No control of agreement
- Communication severely impeded by grammar

---

### 3. Flow and Fluency (30 points)

**Excellent (27-30 points)**
- Natural, smooth delivery
- Minimal hesitation or pauses
- No filler words ("euh," "umm")
- Confident speaking pace
- Natural rhythm and cadence

**Good (24-26 points)**
- Generally smooth delivery with occasional pauses
- Few filler words (1-3 instances)
- Steady pace with minor hesitations
- Good recovery from errors
- Mostly natural rhythm

**Satisfactory (21-23 points)**
- Moderate hesitation and pauses
- Regular use of filler words (4-7 instances)
- Uneven pace
- Some difficulty recovering from errors
- Somewhat stilted rhythm

**Needs Improvement (18-20 points)**
- Frequent long pauses (3+ seconds)
- Extensive filler words (8-12 instances)
- Very slow or rushed pace
- Difficulty maintaining conversation
- Choppy, unnatural delivery

**Unsatisfactory (0-17 points)**
- Constant hesitation and breakdown
- Excessive filler words (13+ instances)
- Unable to maintain communicative flow
- Extremely long pauses
- Speaking appears painful or forced

---

### 4. Extra Credit: Native French Idioms (10 points)

**Points awarded for correct usage of authentic French idioms and expressions:**

**Excellent Usage (9-10 points)**
- Uses 5+ idioms naturally and appropriately
- Idioms specific to France French (not general Francophone)
- Perfect integration into conversation
- Examples: "C'est pas la mer à boire," "Poser un lapin," "Avoir le cafard"

**Good Usage (7-8 points)**
- Uses 3-4 idioms correctly
- Mostly France-specific expressions
- Generally appropriate context
- Minor awkwardness in integration

**Satisfactory Usage (5-6 points)**
- Uses 2 idioms correctly
- May include some generic expressions
- Somewhat forced usage
- Correct meaning but unnatural placement

**Minimal Usage (3-4 points)**
- Uses 1 idiom correctly
- Generic or textbook expression
- Very forced or awkward placement

**No Credit (0-2 points)**
- No idioms used, or
- Idioms used incorrectly

## Common French Idioms for Reference

### Everyday Expressions
- "Avoir le cafard" - to feel down/depressed
- "Coûter les yeux de la tête" - to cost an arm and a leg
- "Poser un lapin" - to stand someone up
- "Casser les pieds à quelqu'un" - to annoy someone

### Difficulty and Effort
- "C'est pas la mer à boire" - it's not that difficult
- "Ce n'est pas sorcier" - it's not rocket science
- "Chercher midi à quatorze heures" - to make things complicated

### Emotions and States
- "Avoir la flemme" - to feel lazy
- "En avoir marre" - to be fed up
- "Tomber dans les pommes" - to faint

### Social Situations
- "Mettre les pieds dans le plat" - to put one's foot in it
- "Faire la grasse matinée" - to sleep in
- "Raconter des salades" - to tell lies/tall tales
